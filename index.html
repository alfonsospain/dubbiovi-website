Welcome to DubbiOvi

"AI-Assisted Audiovisual Translation for Teaching, Research and Knowledge Transfer"

An open-source, local-first environment designed to streamline transcription, translation suggestions, terminology alignment, and accurate video synchronization. Built with academic rigor and privacy at its core.

Download App GitHub Repository Documentation

Powerful Features for Academic AVT

DubbiOvi integrates modern AI tools and translation helper utilities inside a secure offline workstation.

AI Transcription

Convert speech to text using advanced algorithms. Automatically creates timecodes aligned directly with speak changes.

Translation Suggestions

Get context-aware suggestions during dubbing or subtitling. Prompts are optimized for readability limits (CPS).

Terminology Glossary

Import specialized glossaries or dictionaries to enforce translations of domain-specific academic vocabulary.

Video Synchronisation

Synchronize source transcripts and target dubs in frame-accurate real-time. Navigate directly using time-code clicks.

Word Export

Generate formatted Microsoft Word templates (.docx) ideal for academic assignment submissions or printing.

Excel Export

Export structured, sheet-based translations (.xlsx) to compare audio parameters and timing metrics side-by-side.

Local-First Architecture

Guarantees maximum speed, offline compatibility, and security. No databases, accounts, or clouds required.

User Gemini API Keys

Input your Google Gemini credentials directly. Zero developer middleman fees: you pay Google directly for only what you use.

Offline Project Files

Save, copy, or back up translations as simple local JSON files. Perfect for archiving research datasets and sharing with peers.

Why Local-First?

DubbiOvi prioritizes your research, data security, and operational autonomy above all else. Modern web services lock your work in online silos; we keep it on your computer.

Designed specifically to meet the high data protection and privacy standards required by universities, academic journals, and institutional research ethics boards.

The DubbiOvi Translation Workflow

A streamlined, 5-step process tailored for professional audiovisual translators. Click the steps to view details.

Step 1

Import Video Source

Load standard video media containers (MP4, MKV, AVI, WebM) directly from your local filesystem. The system automatically processes the files, extracts audio streams offline, and sets up your translation timeline parameters.

Step 2

AI Audio Transcription

Deploy Google Gemini or local audio transcription algorithms on your media. DubbiOvi generates an accurate, timestamped source-language script, segmented logically according to speaking shifts and silent pauses.

Step 3

Context-Aware Translation

Get instantaneous target-language translation suggestions. The system cross-references active terminology glossaries and fits suggestions to technical requirements like Characters Per Second (CPS) thresholds.

Step 4

Verify and Synchronize

Compare the source speech and final translations in parallel columns. Scrub through the video player to verify that timing, speech pacing, dub loops, and layout constraints synchronize seamlessly with the screen.

Step 5

Multi-Format Export

Export finalized work into structured spreadsheets (Excel, CSV) for research data processing, standard Word files for scripts and printouts, or SRT/VTT for distribution.

About the DubbiOvi Project

DubbiOvi was created and developed by Alfonso C. Rodríguez Fernández-Peña.

The software serves as a modern tool for teaching translation techniques, facilitating translation research datasets, and transferring knowledge from AI audio-processing technologies directly into language classrooms. By enabling local control over proprietary keys and offline execution, the project aims to foster open translation studies globally.
