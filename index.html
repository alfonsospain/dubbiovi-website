Process

The DubbiOvi Translation Workflow

A streamlined, 5-step process tailored for professional audiovisual translators. Click the steps to view details.

Step 1

Import Video Source

Load standard video media containers (MP4, MKV, AVI, WebM) directly from your local filesystem. The system automatically processes the files, extracts audio streams offline, and sets up your translation timeline parameters.

Step 2

AI Audio Transcription

Deploy Google Gemini or local audio transcription algorithms on your media. DubbiOvi generates an accurate, timestamped source-language script, segmented logically according to speaking shifts and silent pauses.

Step 3

Context-Aware Translation

Get instantaneous target-language translation suggestions. The system cross-references active terminology glossaries and fits suggestions to technical requirements like Characters Per Second (CPS) thresholds.

Step 4

Verify and Synchronize

Compare the source speech and final translations in parallel columns. Scrub through the video player to verify that timing, speech pacing, dub loops, and layout constraints synchronize seamlessly with the screen.

Step 5

Multi-Format Export

Export finalized work into structured spreadsheets (Excel, CSV) for research data processing, standard Word files for scripts and printouts, or SRT/VTT for distribution.

About the DubbiOvi Project

DubbiOvi was created and developed by Alfonso C. Rodríguez Fernández-Peña.

The software serves as a modern tool for teaching translation techniques, facilitating translation research datasets, and transferring knowledge from AI audio-processing technologies directly into language classrooms. By enabling local control over proprietary keys and offline execution, the project aims to foster open translation studies globally.
